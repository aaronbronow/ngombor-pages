Ngombor means
having come a long way.

In the Alur language, Ngombor represents the hopefulness born from persistent, dedicated effort. For lasting community development to take place, it requires time, hard work, commitment, and education.

Empowerment & Growth

Our Core Vision

Ngombor Community Development Alliance has a vision to create an empowered and prosperous community in greater Nebbi, in North West Uganda. We are committed to fostering holistic rural community development through direct community engagement and grassroots innovation.

We work to bridge the digital divide in remote areas, equipping families with modern technology tools and sustainable agricultural practices. By operating locally, we ensure that every initiative supports those who need it most.

Diversified Livelihoods

We aim to secure improved incomes and diversified livelihoods for smallholder farm families, moving past single-crop vulnerabilities.

Inclusive Permaculture

Increasing the participation of women, youth, and other underrepresented groups in agricultural innovation and organic food systems.

Enhancing smallholder farmers' resilience to unpredictable weather shocks and protecting local biodiversity through permaculture design.

How We Operationalize

The Three Pillars of Ngombor

Agriculture

Permaculture Model Farm

In May 2025, two dedicated members attended a comprehensive Permaculture Designer course with Permaculture Uganda. We are building on this training to develop localized education, seed sharing, and projects to support community farmers.

  • check_circle Developing a centralized model farm for hands-on ecological training.
  • check_circle Providing community training on disaster risk management and flood mitigation.
  • check_circle Teaching clean, energy-saving technologies like efficient woodstoves.
psychology_alt

"Harmonizing human productivity with ecological wisdom."

Technology

IT Literacy & Local Employment

We aim to challenge the colonial narrative that young people must abandon their rural communities and seek strictly white-collar urban jobs to be successful. In a digital age, software development and IT tools can be accessed anywhere.

  • check_circle IT literacy courses, local internet access, and computer resources.
  • check_circle Partnership with groups like PyLadies Kampala to spark tech career development.
  • check_circle Overcoming isolation: Our first tech workshop required a full day's bus ride from Nebbi to Kampala.
terminal

"In a digital world, opportunity should have no geographical limits."

Finance

Autonomy & Mitigation

We empower members economically through community savings and cooperative soft loans. Building a resilient savings culture allows farmers to navigate climate emergencies and health events without falling into debt cycles.

  • check_circle Autonomy: Soft loans are funded directly by members' savings, eliminating predatory bank rates.
  • check_circle Mitigation: Solid cushions against crop failures and unpredictable extreme weather events.
  • check_circle Support: Holistic psychosocial aid to families navigating challenging community losses.
savings

"Building independent safety nets, managed and sustained by the community."

The Local Context

Why Nebbi District Matters

Greater Nebbi, located in remote North West Uganda along the Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC) border, faces considerable socioeconomic hurdles. Yet, it holds an energetic, young population eager to build a better future.

78%
Under 30 Years Old

Uganda has one of the youngest populations globally, presenting massive human potential.

75%
Unemployed / Underemployed

Three-quarters of rural youth struggle to secure reliable, meaningful livelihoods.

High
Socioeconomic Strain

The Greater North region recovers from historic LRA conflicts, facing persistent poverty.

Deeply Rooted, Locally Driven
